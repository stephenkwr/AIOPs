Aurora Supply Co. accepts Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Discover, PayPal, Apple Pay, and Google Pay at checkout. We do not accept cash on delivery, personal checks, or direct bank transfers.

**When your card is charged**
Placing an order puts an authorization hold on your card for the full order amount. For items that are in stock, we capture (actually charge) that amount within 1 hour of the order being placed. If your order includes a backordered or pre-order item, that portion is not captured until the item ships from our warehouse — typically 10-15 business days later — not when you originally checked out. This means an order that splits into two shipments can show up as two separate charges on your statement, each dated to its own dispatch.

**Invoices and receipts**
A PDF invoice is emailed automatically within 30 minutes of each shipment dispatching. You can also find it anytime under My Account > Order History > select the order > "View Invoice"; invoices remain available there for 7 years. If you need documentation before dispatch (for example, to expense a pre-order), contact support and we can issue a pro-forma invoice.

**Failed or declined payments**
If checkout declines your card, the order is placed on "Payment Failed" status and your items are held for 48 hours. You'll receive an email with a "Retry Payment" link to update your card and complete the charge. If payment still hasn't gone through after 48 hours, the order auto-cancels and the items are released back into inventory. The most common causes are insufficient funds, an expired card, or your bank's fraud filter blocking an online purchase — calling your card issuer usually resolves it fastest.

**Do we store your card?**
No. Card numbers are tokenized by our PCI DSS Level 1 certified payment processor; Aurora's systems never see or store the full 16-digit number. We retain only the last 4 digits, expiry date, and card brand so you can recognize saved cards under Account Settings > Payment Methods, where you can remove them anytime. A saved card is also automatically removed after 3 consecutive failed charge attempts as a security precaution.
